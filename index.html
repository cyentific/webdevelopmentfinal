Mission

Our Goal is to help individuals in creative industries learn the effects of social media on creativity.

Pros Vs. Cons

Pros

Expanding Knowledge

With an innumerable population, social media allows users to connect and share knowledge across a variety of countries, cultures, and subjects. Users are easily able to find information about their interests and can verify that information through a variety of sources.

Finding Community

With most people having access to the internet, and thus social media, users are able to connect with others and form communities with shared interests that can range from religion, gaming preferences, identity, to virtually anything. Through community, people are able to feel less alone.

Source of Inspiration

Social media can be an incredibly powerful tool to gather references and relevant sources of inspiration for potential work. With the help of intelligent algorithms, all apps recommend users content based on the user’s interest. This makes the sorting of content more efficient for users, as well as allowing users to find relevant content that they might not have seen otherwise.

Promoting your work

Social media acts most importantly as a platform for commerce, marketing, and connections. Creative users are able to share their work on platforms like Instagram to present their portfolio and find their audiences. With exposure, creatives can be able to find clients for their services or products.

Getting Feedback

With a public profile, creative users are able to post their content publicly, allowing virtually anybody to respond. This can be beneficial as it can allow the user to gain constructive criticism to improve their work, however this can also work negatively to allow people to comment hate. It is incredibly important for the user to monitor the activity of their account, in order to ensure that they are not a victim of hate.

Cons

Oversaturation

Due to the large volume of users and hence content per user, there is a lot of diversity in both the quality and content of work that is uploaded on social media. As a result, while algorithms can help users find desired content, users still have to spend time sorting through the options presented to them to find desired references.

Quantification of Creativity

Because social media is innately built on the frameworks of likes, shares, and comments. Creatives often have to deal with and accept the quantification of their work. It can easily become a comparison game with others as each post has publicly available data that represents the work.

Theft of Ideas

As it was mentioned, social media can serve as a great resource for finding references however this comes at the cost that a user’s posts and content can easily become another person’s reference. With this risk comes the possibility of a creative user’s design or content being copied by an external party. This evidently raises concerns about intellectual property, but this is a risk that user’s need to evaluate before sharing non-copyrighted, non-patented, or unprotected work.

Losing Sense of Real Life

While social media can allow individuals to connect with other individuals across the world, this can easily take away time for users to interact with people in real life. As a result there is a loss in real life connection.

Time waste

Apps are designed to manipulate users into addiction through predictive algorithms and attention psychology. This can cause users to waste a lot of time.

Online Hate

With such a diverse audience and reach, social media allows users from all sorts of interests to interact. If anything apps try to mingle users from opposing views to create tension and virality. As a result, social media can quickly foster toxic and harmful online environments, like online hate, death threats, and more.
